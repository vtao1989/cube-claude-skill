# Matrix estimation

We will consider the solution of the generalized minimization problem
given by

![](GUID-BA9D67F2-8C33-432C-89FE-3E625B2470CE-low.png)Equation 1

where A is the route choice probability matrix,
X is the OD matrix to be estimated, and
X0 is the initial OD matrix. F is a
function of measure between simulated volumes given by the product AX and their
observed counterparts
b. G is a background term measuring the distance
between the current OD matrix
X and the initial estimate
X0. We note that in practice, A represents
a 5-D space mapped to a 2-D matrix,
X represents a 3D space mapped to a 1D vector, and b
represents a 2D space mapped to a 1D vector. The dimensions of
A are given by

![](GUID-36EAD6E5-C4E1-4E79-9098-C01F23E16A4B-low.png)Equation 2

We will use the capital letters to represent the cardinality (or size)
of the dimension, and the lower case to represent an individual entry of the
dimension. Note that for dimension sizes we should always have I equal to J and
D equal to T, but will use them individually for clarity. In this manner, the
2D representation of
A is found via the transformation

![](GUID-D72087D7-18AC-4F83-8E04-F0C5CA0DCEC0-low.png)Equation 3

In a similar manner, the dimensions of
b(k,t) are mapped just as the first dimension of the
mapping of
A, and
X(i,j,d) as the second dimension of the mapping of A.
The special case of static estimation is found when D and T are both equal to
1, and we effectively reduce these dimensions of time from the models.
